Basics - Investing in stocks means buying shares of ownership in a public company. Those small shares are known as the company’s stock, and by investing in that stock, you’re hoping the company grows and performs well over time. When that happens, your shares may become more valuable, and other investors may be willing to buy them from you for more than you paid for them. Investing in stocks is a long term game, and those who play it like that will be rewarded!

1) For your purposes we are assuming that you'd like to invest in stocks on your own. Firstly, you want to open a stock brokerage account for you to be able to trade the stocks. There are many options, a good option to chose is TD WebBroker Online Trading.

2) After opening an account and deposit the amount of money you would like to start with, you have to decide where you want to put that money into use. You can either chose to invest in one companies stock or many through mutual funds or index funds which is a fund that hold stocks of many companies like the S&P 500 index. This index basically tracks the whole US economy, but there are also other options where you could buy index funds that focus on small, eco-friendly, or tech companies.

3) Set a budget for how much you would like to spend on you account, but know that the more you put in now the better you will be in the long term. When budgeting you can set up something where you put aside a certain amount each month to contribute to you account.

4) Focus on the long term, don't get worried about the short term price. Focus on investing into companies that you think will thrive in decades from now, you will thank yourself then for doing so. When recessions happen, which is garenteed eventually, take that oppertunity to buy stocks at a great discount.

5) "Be fearful when others are greedy, and greedy when others are fearful." This is one famous quote by Warren Buffet, a regular guy who took that quote into action accumalting his wealth currently into the top 5 wealthiest people.
